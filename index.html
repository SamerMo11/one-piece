Base Gear

When does Luffy's base form first appear?

Anime: Episode 1
Manga: Chapter 1

The basic Devil Fruit powers he exhibits are not considered an official "Gear 1," but you can take this as Luffy's baseline.

Gear 2

When does Luffy's Gear 2 first appear?

Anime: Episode 272
Manga: Chapter 387

This form grants Luffy greater speed, strength and mobility in utilizing the fact that every part of Luffy is rubbery, even his blood vessels.

Gear 3

When does Luffy's Gear 3 first appear?

Anime: Episode 288
Manga: Chapter 403

This is a technique Luffy breaks out when he needs raw strength, with his inflated fists able to deal insane damage, though they are quite slow-moving.

Gear 4
( Boundman/Bounceman )

When does Luffy's Gear 4 first appear?

Anime: Episode 726
Manga: Chapter 784

This form is activated by Luffy covering his arm in Armament Haki, biting and blowing air into it and inflating his muscular structure to increase his muscle mass (specifically his upper body) as well as inflating his fist bones, topping it all off with speeding up his blood flow and armamenting his arms and legs.

Gear 4
( Tankman )

When does Luffy's Tankman form first appear?

Anime: Episode 806
Manga: Chapter 842

By inflating his top half into a spherical shape and covering most of his body in Armament Haki, Luffy is able to body basically any hit Cracker throws at him. As its namesake suggests, this form makes Luffy a tank, creating an immovable object and unbreakable shield in one form.

Gear 4
( Snakeman )

When does Luffy's Snakeman form first appear?

Anime: Episode 870
Manga: Chapter 894

The Armament Haki on his limbs, increased muscle mass and increased blood flow are all there, but this form favors speed and control instead of being a hulking mass of muscle. With just a slight increase of muscle mass and arm size, Snakeman is much sleeker, with the focus being on what his shielded arms can do.

Gear 5
( Awakening )

When does Luffy's Gear 5 first appear?

Anime: Episode 1071
Manga: Chapter 1044

Similar to the abilities of a Paramecia Devil Fruit’s awakening, this form allows Luffy to treat anything he touches as though it were made of rubber, like him. However, that’s not the main attraction of Gear 5. Before it was known as the Gum Gum Fruit, it was known as the Human-Human Fruit, Model: Nika — meaning it’s a Devil Fruit that grants the user the powers of the Sun God Nika, a deity said to have “the most ridiculous power in the world,” in the form of a rubber body.

When the power of the Gum Gum Fruit is awakened, Nika comes alive in Luffy, turning his clothes and hair white, giving him environment-manipulating abilities and increasing the capabilities of his rubber body. It results in Luffy being able to fight like a classic rubber-hose cartoon character, letting him turn giant, grab and throw lightning, deflect nearly any attack, and do a dozen other things.

It is the power of true freedom, though it comes at a cost — Luffy practically becomes an aged husk after wearing himself out using Gear 5.

In the One Piece manga, a Yonko is one of the four most powerful pirate captains in the world. They are considered to be the most formidable and influential pirates in the seas. The term "Yonko" translates to "Four Emperors" in Japanese. As for whether we will see all four Yonko in one story arc, I don't have information on future developments in the One Piece manga as my knowledge is based on information available before 2021. However, it's worth noting that the interactions between the Yonko are a significant aspect of the One Piece storyline, and the possibility of all four Yonko appearing in one story arc cannot be ruled out.

Storyline

An alternate version of Earth, and one that is currently in the midst of the 'Golden Age of Pirates'. Ruthless cut-throat pirates rule the seas, and only the strongest have the chance to lay claim to the mythical treasure known as 'One Piece' that was left behind by the greatest pirate of them all Gold Roger. Years after the death of Gold Roger, a young boy by the name of Monkey D. Luffy has dreams of raising his own crew, finding One Piece, and declaring himself as Pirate King. After eating a devil fruit that grants Luffy the power to make his body like rubber, it gives him enormous strength and agility. When Luffy finally comes of age, he sets sail from Foosha Village in East Blue and sets upon his grand adventure to become the next Pirate King.
